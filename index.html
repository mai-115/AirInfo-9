What is IATA?

IATA stands for the International Air Transport Association. It is a global trade association for airlines, and it assigns unique two-letter codes (IATA codes) to airlines and airports worldwide. These codes are used to identify airlines (like AI for Air India, 6E for IndiGo) and airports in flight schedules, tickets, and data systems.

  • Airline IATA Code: A two-letter code (e.g., AI, 6E, SG) that uniquely identifies an airline.
  • Why is it important? It ensures accuracy and consistency in airline and airport identification across the world.

When searching for flights, use the correct IATA code for the airline you are interested in.
